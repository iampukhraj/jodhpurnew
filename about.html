Jodhpur, the second largest city in Rajasthan, is popularly known as the Blue City. This nickname is particularly apt as most of the architecture - including forts, palaces, temples, havelis, and even residential houses - are painted in striking shades of blue.

The city is dominated by the magnificent Mehrangarh Fort, which stands atop a 125-meter high hill. This imposing structure features thick walls, intricate carvings, and seven gates that lead into the ancient walled city. The fort's museum houses an exquisite collection of palanquins, howdahs, royal cradles, miniatures, musical instruments, costumes, and furniture.

Jodhpur is also renowned for the Marwari or Malani horse breed, known for its distinctive inward-turning ear tips. The city's vibrant markets offer traditional textiles, metalware, and handicrafts, while its cuisine features local specialties like Mirchi Bada and Mawa Kachori.
